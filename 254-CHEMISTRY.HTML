Chemistry is the science of matter and its transformations, essential for medicine, engineering, and understanding the material world.

Syllabus Breakdown

First Year (Chemistry-I)

  • Atomic Structure: Quantum numbers, Electronic configuration
  • Classification of Elements: Periodic properties, Modern periodic table
  • Chemical Bonding: Ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds
  • States of Matter: Gases, liquids, and solids
  • Stoichiometry: Mole concept, Redox reactions
  • Thermodynamics: Laws of thermodynamics, Enthalpy
  • Chemical Equilibrium: Le Chatelier's principle
  • Hydrogen & Its Compounds: Water, Hydrogen peroxide
  • s-Block Elements: Alkali and alkaline earth metals

Second Year (Chemistry-II)

  • Solid State: Crystal lattices, Unit cells
  • Solutions: Colligative properties
  • Electrochemistry: Conductance, Batteries
  • Chemical Kinetics: Rate of reaction, Order of reaction
  • Surface Chemistry: Adsorption, Catalysis
  • General Principles of Metallurgy: Extraction processes
  • p-Block Elements: Group 13-18 elements
  • d & f Block Elements: Transition elements
  • Coordination Compounds: Werner's theory, Isomerism
  • Haloalkanes & Haloarenes: Preparation, Properties
  • Organic Compounds: Alcohols, Phenols, Ethers
  • Biomolecules: Carbohydrates, Proteins, Vitamins
  • Polymers: Classification, Polymerization
  • Chemistry in Everyday Life: Drugs, Medicines
