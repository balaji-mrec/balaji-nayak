Government Jobs After Intermediate

Excellent Government Job Opportunities After 12th Grade

Railway Jobs

Railway Group D

  • Posts: Track Maintainer, Helper, Gateman
  • Eligibility: 10th/12th pass with ITI certificate
  • Age Limit: 18-33 years (relaxation applicable)
  • Selection Process: Computer Based Test + Physical Efficiency Test

RRB Group D Exam Pattern

  • Mathematics - 25 questions
  • General Intelligence - 30 questions
  • General Science - 25 questions
  • General Awareness - 20 questions
Approx Salary: ₹18,000 - ₹22,000 per month

Railway Apprentice

  • Posts: Technician, Fitter, Electrician
  • Eligibility: 10th pass + ITI in relevant trade
  • Age Limit: 15-24 years
  • Selection Process: Written Test + Document Verification

RRB Apprentice Exam

  • Trade Theory - 50 questions
  • Trade Practical - 25 questions
  • General Awareness - 25 questions
Stipend: ₹7,000 - ₹12,000 per month during training

Other Government Jobs

Defence Jobs

  • Indian Army Soldier (GD): 12th pass with 45% marks
  • Indian Navy Sailor: 12th with Maths & Physics
  • Indian Air Force (Airmen): 12th with 50% marks
  • BSF/CRPF/SSB: Various constable positions

Physical Standards

  • Height: 165-170 cm (varies by post)
  • Chest: 77-82 cm (expandable)
  • Running: 1.6km in 6-7 minutes
Starting Salary: ₹21,700 - ₹25,500 per month

SSC & State Govt Jobs

  • SSC CHSL: LDC, DEO, Postal Assistant
  • SSC GD Constable: Police, CAPF, CISF
  • State Police: Constable, Warder, Jail Guard
  • Forest Department: Forest Guard, Watcher

Common Exam Pattern

  • General Awareness - 25 questions
  • General Intelligence - 25 questions
  • Mathematics - 25 questions
  • English/Hindi - 25 questions
Approx Salary: ₹18,000 - ₹25,000 per month

Preparation Strategy

Time Management: Create a daily study schedule with 2-3 hours dedicated to exam preparation. Focus more on General Awareness and Mathematics.
Study Material: Focus on NCERT books (10th level) for basic concepts. Use previous year question papers to understand the exam pattern.
Physical Fitness: For defence and police jobs, start physical training early - focus on running, push-ups, and high jumps.
Current Affairs: Read newspapers daily and make notes of important government schemes, sports events, and awards.
Mock Tests: Take online mock tests weekly to improve speed and accuracy. Analyze mistakes to avoid repetition.
