GUJCET 2026 Overview

GUJCET (Gujarat Common Entrance Test) is the state-level entrance exam for admission to B.Tech and other professional courses in Gujarat. Conducted by the Gujarat Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board (GSEB), GUJCET is the gateway to top engineering colleges in the state.

Detailed Subject-wise Syllabus

The GUJCET syllabus is based on Gujarat State Board and NCERT Class 11 & 12 for Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics:

Mathematics

  • Sets, Relations, and Functions
  • Complex Numbers, Quadratic Equations
  • Permutations, Combinations, Probability
  • Trigonometry, Matrices, Determinants
  • Differentiation, Integration, Differential Equations
  • Straight Lines, Circles, Conic Sections

Physics

  • Units & Measurements, Kinematics
  • Laws of Motion, Work, Energy, Power
  • Thermodynamics, Oscillations, Waves
  • Electrostatics, Current Electricity
  • Magnetism, Optics, Modern Physics

Chemistry

  • Atomic Structure, Chemical Bonding
  • Thermodynamics, Equilibrium
  • Electrochemistry, Surface Chemistry
  • Organic Chemistry: Hydrocarbons, Biomolecules
  • Inorganic Chemistry: Periodic Table, Coordination Compounds

Strategic Preparation Plan

Follow this 3-phase plan for GUJCET 2026 success:

Phase 1: Foundation Building (Jan-Feb)

  • Revise all concepts from Gujarat Board/NCERT books
  • Daily practice of MCQs for each subject
  • Focus on weak areas and time management

Phase 2: Practice & Mock Tests (Mar)

  • Solve previous years' GUJCET papers
  • Take weekly offline mock tests using OMR sheets
  • Analyze mistakes and improve speed

Phase 3: Final Revision (Apr)

  • Quick revision of formulas and key concepts
  • Focus on high-weightage chapters
  • Attempt full-length mock exams
Recommended Daily Schedule (6-8 hours):
7:00-10:00 AM: Theory | 11:00-1:00 PM: Practice | 2:00-4:00 PM: Mock Tests | 5:00-7:00 PM: Revision

Time Management Strategy

"Attempt easy questions first, then move to moderate and tough ones. Use OMR sheets for practice to avoid bubbling errors."

Exam Pattern & Marking Scheme

GUJCET 2026 is an offline OMR-based test with the following pattern:

Paper Subject No. of Questions Marks per Question
Paper I Physics & Chemistry 80 (40+40) 1
Paper II Mathematics 40 1
  • Total Questions: 120 (80 Physics & Chemistry, 40 Mathematics)
  • Duration: 120 minutes (60 min for Paper I, 60 min for Paper II)
  • Marking: +1 for correct, -0.25 for incorrect

Exam Strategy

"Attempt all easy questions first, manage time for both papers, and avoid spending too long on any one question."

- Smit Patel (GUJCET Topper 2025)

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common queries about GUJCET 2026 preparation and admission process:

What is GUJCET?

GUJCET is the Gujarat Common Entrance Test for admission to B.Tech and other professional courses in Gujarat.

Who is eligible for GUJCET 2026?

Candidates who have passed or appeared for 12th with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics from a recognized board are eligible. Minimum 45% aggregate in PCM.

Is there negative marking in GUJCET?

Yes, +1 for correct answer and -0.25 for incorrect answer. No marks for unattempted questions.

How to prepare for GUJCET?

Focus on Gujarat Board/NCERT textbooks, solve previous year papers, take mock tests, and revise regularly. Practice OMR sheets for accuracy.

What are the top colleges through GUJCET?

LDCE Ahmedabad, Nirma University, DDU Nadiad, VGEC Ahmedabad, MSU Baroda, and other top government and private colleges in Gujarat participate in GUJCET counseling.

How are seats allotted in GUJCET?

Seats are allotted based on GUJCET rank, preferences, and seat availability through online counseling by GSEB.
