Physics is the fundamental science of matter and energy, essential for engineering, technology, and understanding the natural world.

Syllabus Breakdown

First Year (Physics-I)

  • Physical World: Nature of physical laws, Physics and technology
  • Units and Measurements: Fundamental and derived units, Dimensional analysis
  • Motion in a Straight Line: Kinematics, Uniform and non-uniform motion
  • Motion in a Plane: Projectile motion, Uniform circular motion
  • Laws of Motion: Newton's laws, Friction, Circular motion
  • Work, Energy and Power: Kinetic and potential energy, Power
  • System of Particles and Rotational Motion: Center of mass, Moment of inertia
  • Oscillations: Simple harmonic motion, Pendulums
  • Waves: Wave motion, Sound waves, Doppler effect

Second Year (Physics-II)

  • Electric Charges and Fields: Coulomb's law, Electric field, Gauss's law
  • Electrostatic Potential and Capacitance: Potential, Capacitors, Dielectrics
  • Current Electricity: Ohm's law, Resistivity, Kirchhoff's laws
  • Moving Charges and Magnetism: Magnetic field, Biot-Savart law, Ampere's law
  • Magnetism and Matter: Bar magnet, Earth's magnetism
  • Electromagnetic Induction: Faraday's law, Lenz's law, AC generator
  • Alternating Current: AC circuits, LCR circuits, Power factor
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Displacement current, EM spectrum
  • Ray Optics: Reflection, Refraction, Optical instruments
  • Wave Optics: Huygens principle, Interference, Diffraction
  • Dual Nature of Radiation: Photoelectric effect, de Broglie wavelength
  • Atoms: Atomic models, Spectral series
  • Nuclei: Nuclear properties, Radioactivity, Nuclear reactions
  • Semiconductor Electronics: Diodes, Transistors, Logic gates

Expert Study Tips

Conceptual Understanding: Focus on understanding fundamental concepts rather than rote memorization
NCERT First: Master NCERT textbooks thoroughly before moving to reference books
Weightage Strategy: Prioritize chapters with high marks weightage like Optics and Electromagnetism
Numerical Practice: Solve at least 30 numerical problems daily from different chapters
Derivation Practice: Practice all important derivations with proper diagrams and explanations
Experiment Diagrams: Memorize important experimental setups and circuit diagrams
