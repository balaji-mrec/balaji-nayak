INTERMEDIATE ENGLISH GUIDE Study Materials

Mastering English is essential for academic success and opens doors to global opportunities in every field.

First Year Syllabus

Textbook: English Reader

  • Unit I: Personality Development (Attitude, Gratitude)
  • Unit II: Wit and Humor (The Dear Departed, The Brave Potter)
  • Unit III: Human Relations (A Tale of Three Villages, The Journey)
  • Unit IV: Films and Theatre (The Never-Never Nest, A Ghost's Story)
  • Unit V: Sports and Wellness (The Winning Team, The Concert)
  • Unit VI: Ethics and Values (The Bed of Arrows, The Merchant of Venice)

Language Components

  • Reading Comprehension: Unseen passages with questions
  • Vocabulary: Synonyms, Antonyms, One-word substitutes
  • Grammar: Tenses, Prepositions, Articles, Reported Speech
  • Creative Writing: Essay writing, Letter writing, Report writing
  • Study Skills: Note-making, Summarizing, Reference skills
  • Oral Communication: Group discussions, Presentations

Study Tips for English

Daily Reading: Read English newspapers, magazines or novels for at least 30 minutes daily to improve comprehension and vocabulary.
Writing Practice: Write one essay/letter/report daily on different topics to improve writing skills and speed.
Speaking Practice: Engage in English conversations with friends/family to improve fluency and confidence.
Grammar Drills: Complete at least 20 grammar exercises daily from different topics (tenses, prepositions, etc.)
Vocabulary Building: Learn 10 new words daily with meanings and usage in sentences.
Time Management: Practice previous years' question papers within time limits to improve speed and accuracy.
